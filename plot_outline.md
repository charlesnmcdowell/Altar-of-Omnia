# Altar of Omnia — The Unbound Path

## Roadmap

**Chapter 1: The Bio-Paperweight and the $190 Suicide Note**
*   **The Setting:** Back in their shared apartment after a standard 30-hour work week. The glow of the pods is the only light as they finalize their spawn coordinates.
*   **The Debate:** Kenji pushes for the Iron Citadel to secure starter quests and basic steel. Lyra is adamant about the Whispering Taiga. She argues that by the time they walk from a city to the woods, the corporate "whales" will have already bought up the Druid territory. They agree to risk the Unbound spawn to get ahead of the curve.
*   **The Sync:** As they settle into the gel-beds, Kenji manually overrides the safety protocols, locking his Pain Sensitivity to 80%. He doesn't tell Lyra; he just wants the edge that raw feedback provides.
*   **The Transition:** The hum of Chattanooga fades into the violent, freezing wind of a forest that doesn't want them there.

**Chapter 2: Iron-Oak and Iron Will**
*   **The Landing:** The Whispering Taiga is a sensory overload. The smell of rot and damp pine is cloying, and every shadow feels like a predator. They are hyper-aware that without a Bind-Point, one mistake means their avatars are gone.
*   **The Crafting:** Rejecting the "Item Subscriptions" found in cities, Kenji harvests a fallen Iron-Oak branch. He uses a sharp stone to shave it into a Bokken. It’s heavy, balanced, and—for now—their only hope.
*   **The Lethality Event:** A Giant Weaving-Spider drops from the canopy. Kenji’s high-pain reflexes allow him to track the micro-vibrations in the air, parrying the chitinous legs.
*   **The Chaos:** Lyra, having no spells, resorts to desperation tactics—hurling heavy rocks to distract the beast. Kenji takes a poisoned graze to the ribs to keep the spider's fangs away from her.

**Chapter 3: The Huddle and the Breakthrough**
*   **The Shelter:** They retreat to a hollowed-out tree for an 8-hour in-game night. The darkness is absolute and lethal.
*   **Skill Development:** They spend these hours in frantic practice.
    *   **Kenji:** The system recognizes his real-world Kendo forms. He is awarded [Flowing Strike] and [Kinetic Deflection], turning his wooden stick into a precision weapon.
    *   **Lyra:** In the panic of Kenji’s fever, she experiments with "Focusing" on the moss and roots. She manifests a rudimentary [Vital Sap] heal and a stinging [Nettle Lash].
*   **The Cost:** Despite the new skills, they remain "glass cannons"—no armor, no safety net.

**Chapter 4: The Shadow of the Whale**
*   **The Observation:** While moving through the brush, they spot another player. He is a "Whale"—covered in shimmering Enchanted Plate and wielding a glowing blade. He looks like a god compared to their rags and wood.
*   **The Combat:** They hide and watch as the Whale fights a Forest Behemoth. His gear is superior, but he moves sluggishly, relying on system-assists. He kills the beast but fails to see the "Logic" of the woods, eventually wandering deeper into the thicket, unable to find the trail.
*   **The Discovery:** Lyra spots a squirrel with strangely intelligent eyes. Instead of ignoring it, she uses her intuition to attempt [Animal Speech]. The "squirrel" is a Druid scout in form; it leads them toward a hidden trail the armored player missed.

**Chapter 5: The Trial of the Blight-Guardian**
*   **The Trap:** They witness a lethal environmental trap trigger nearby. A different player—one they don't recognize—is caught by shifting "Glass-Vines." Because of the low-pain settings and lack of awareness, the player is shredded and vanishes instantly. They don't know if he was "deleted," but the message is clear: this is the end of the line.
*   **The Boss:** They face the Blight-Guardian at the Grove’s entrance.
*   **The Synergy:** Kenji uses his 80% sensitivity to parry 1-hit-kill rot-spells, while Lyra uses her new nature spells to root the Guardian in place.
*   **The Bind:** They defeat the beast and are led to the Great Heart-Tree. The notification: [STATUS: BOUND].
*   **The Logout:** The 8-hour pod timer ends. They wake up back in the apartment, exhausted but finally "Owners" of their own progress.

## The Antagonist: SABLE

**Player Name:** Sable
**Real Name:** Margaux Ihejirika
**Class Path:** Blightweaver (Unique — Necromancer/Corruption Hybrid)
**Status:** UNBOUND (like Kenji and Lyra — no Citadel safety net)

**Background:** Margaux is a 28-year-old former biotech researcher who was laid off when her AI replacement outperformed her entire department in three weeks. She entered Omnia bitter, brilliant, and looking for a system she could control instead of being controlled by. She spawned solo in the Taiga two weeks before Kenji and Lyra, chose Unbound for the same reason they did — she refused to be a tenant.

**How She Found the Blight:** While exploring deep corruption zones that other players avoided, Sable discovered something the druids never did — the Blight isn't just a threat. It's an **unclaimed system.** A dormant magical infrastructure left behind by a previous civilization, waiting for someone to authenticate as its operator. The repeating lattice, the compiler-like propagation — it was always designed to be *used.* It just needed a user.

**The Unique Opportunity:** Sable found an ancient terminal — a Blight Nexus buried beneath the cave — and the system offered her a choice:

[UNIQUE CLASS PATH DETECTED: BLIGHTWEAVER]
[Description: Command the dormant corruption network. Rewrite. Reclaim. Rule.]
[Warning: This path is incompatible with Grove allegiance.]
[Warning: Accepting this path marks you as a CORRUPTION SOVEREIGN.]
[Accept?]

She accepted.

**Her Philosophy (The Jason Parallel):** Sable isn't a villain who chose evil for fun. She has a worldview:
- The Grove druids are *gatekeepers* — they hoard nature magic behind trials and pacts, deciding who deserves power. Sound familiar? It's the same corporate gatekeeping from the real world, dressed in leaves instead of contracts.
- The Blight is *open-source power.* It doesn't ask you to prove your character. It doesn't judge your intent. It reformats everything equally. That's not corruption — that's democracy.
- She sees herself as a liberator. She's building a domain where power isn't locked behind someone else's moral test.

**Her Flaw:** The Blight isn't neutral. It *is* rewriting her. Every day she holds the Nexus, her decision-making gets colder, her empathy thins, her "liberation" philosophy starts looking more like conquest. She doesn't fully realize she's being corrupted by her own system — the compiler doesn't just reformat the forest. It reformats the operator.

**Combat Style:** She doesn't fight directly. She commands. Raised creatures, shaped terrain, corruption fields that debuff and drain. Her strongest assets are the raised druid leadership — powerful nature mages rewired to serve the Blight network. She's a raid boss who sends lieutenants first.

**Her Domain:** The cave system is her base of operations. The deeper you go, the more structured and intentional the corruption becomes — hallways of shaped bone-coral, bioluminescent rot that functions as lighting, root-networks repurposed as communication lines. It looks less like a cave and more like someone's *headquarters.*

## Chapter 5: The Throat of It (8–10 scenes)

**Chapter Goal:** Enter cave → discover the horror → fight first raised druid → barely survive → realize the scope of the threat.

### Scene Breakdown:

**Descent**
- Cave interior shifts from natural rock to shaped corruption. Walls pulse with the lattice pattern. The air hums.
- Kenji's Mark amplifies — everything in here knows they've arrived.
- Boot prints from the Citadel party are visible. Then they stop. Scuff marks. Signs of a fight. Discarded gear. No bodies.

**The Architecture**
- The cave transitions from organic to *designed.* Bone-coral pillars. Bioluminescent rot as lighting. Root-networks running along walls like conduit.
- Kenji's gamer brain fires: `This isn't a dungeon. Someone lives here. Someone is building.`
- Lyra's Purifier sense floods — the corruption is denser, more organized, and it has a *heartbeat* deeper inside.

**The Trophy Hall**
- They find the missing druids.
- Not dead. Not alive. Standing in alcoves carved from the cave wall, posed like specimens in a museum. Eyes open. Bark-armor fused with corruption lattice. Faint green glow still in their eyes — nature magic imprisoned, not destroyed.
- Lyra's reaction: horror. Visceral. These were her people's leaders. She can feel them — dimly, like hearing someone scream through soundproofing.
- UI notification: [QUEST UPDATE: The Missing Circle — STATUS: LOCATED (Corrupted)]

**The First Raised Druid**
- One of the alcove druids activates — steps forward, staff in hand, eyes flickering between green (nature) and pale (blight).
- It fights with druid magic *inverted* — corrupted root spells, blighted thorns, nature magic running the wrong direction.
- This is a mid-boss. It's strong. It knows Grove techniques because it *was* Grove leadership.
- Kenji and Lyra fight desperately. Kenji's Bloodtide Counter gets its first real test. Lyra's Purifier ability lets her partially disrupt the corruption controlling the druid, creating openings.
- They win — barely. The druid crumbles. The green glow fades last, like the real person saying goodbye.
- Lyra is shaken. Kenji holds the line emotionally.

**The Voice**
- A voice echoes through the cave. Not a monster. A *player.*
- "You broke my favorite one. That took me three days to calibrate."
- Sable doesn't appear. Just speaks through the corruption network — her voice coming from the walls, the roots, the lattice itself.
- She's curious about them. Claimants? Grovebound? A Purifier? She's impressed. And concerned.
- She offers them a deal: leave now. She'll let them go. The Grove can keep its little ward. She just wants the deep territory.
- Lyra refuses.
- Sable sighs. "I was like you. Angry at the right things. Then I found something better than anger."

**The Citadel Party**
- They find the whale's group — or what's left. Two of the three are dead (permadeath — Unbound? Or did Sable break their tethers?). The third is alive, barely, hiding in a crevice, terrified.
- The survivor tells them: the woman controls everything in here. The walls. The creatures. The *dead.* She offered them the same deal. They attacked her. She didn't even fight them herself.
- This establishes Sable's power level: she solo-wiped a geared Citadel party without direct combat.

**The Retreat**
- More raised druids activate. Two at once. Then a third.
- Kenji and Lyra can't hold. They retreat — fighting backwards, covering each other, Lyra sealing corridors behind them with Verdant Latch.
- They escape the cave with the survivor.
- Cliffhanger: Sable's voice follows them out. "You'll come back. Purifiers always do. It's in the job description."

**End Chapter 5:** They know the enemy. A player. A system. A philosophy. And they're not strong enough.

## Chapter 6: The Weight of the Circle (8–10 scenes)

**Chapter Goal:** Return to Grove → deliver devastating news → emotional fallout → Grove druids commit to action → Kenji and Lyra receive formal training and domain authority.

### Scene Breakdown:

**The Return**
- They limp back to the Grove with the Citadel survivor (who becomes a minor recurring character — humbled, useful for intel).
- The Grove reacts to their wounds, to the corruption residue on them, to the news they carry.

**The Report**
- They tell the Grove woman (and the remaining druids) what they found. The leadership isn't missing. They're weapons now.
- The druids' reaction: grief, fury, denial, then cold resolve.
- The young druid who covered Eira's nameplate in Chapter 3 breaks down. Others hold him.
- This is the emotional floor of the book. The Grove lost its family and now has to plan their destruction.

**Lyra's Reckoning**
- Lyra privately processes what she felt in the cave — the imprisoned druids, the nature magic screaming under corruption.
- She confronts the Grove woman: "You trained for years under Eira. You never unlocked Purifier. I did it in two days. Why?"
- The Grove woman's answer is honest and painful: "Because you're not afraid of what the Blight is. I am."
- This deepens Lyra's understanding of her own power — Purifier isn't just a classification. It's a willingness to *touch* corruption without flinching. Most druids can't.

**Kenji's System Mapping**
- Kenji works with the Citadel survivor to map what they saw. The cave layout. Sable's behavior. The raised druids' combat patterns.
- He identifies the weakness: the raised druids are running on the Blight lattice, not their own will. If you can sever the lattice connection, they collapse. The problem is the lattice is everywhere inside the cave.
- Except where a Purifier cleans it.
- The strategy crystallizes: Lyra is the key. She can carve a clean zone through the corruption, severing Sable's control network room by room. Kenji defends her while she works.
- But Lyra's Purifier ability isn't strong enough yet. She needs more attunement. More power.

**The Offer — Grove Domain**
- The Grove woman makes a decision that the remaining druids debate fiercely.
- She offers Kenji and Lyra something unprecedented: **Grove Domain Authority.**
- Not just citizenship. Ownership. Stewardship. The Grove recognizes them as its champions — tied to the Heart-Tree, empowered by the ward network.

[DOMAIN AUTHORITY GRANTED: Verdant Grove]
[Effect: Attunement amplified within Grove territory]
[Effect: Ward network responds to Domain holders]
[Effect: Nature magic costs reduced (significant)]
[Warning: Domain holders share the Grove's fate. If the Grove falls, you fall.]

- Kenji: "So we went from tenants to landlords. With a mortgage that kills us if we default."
- This is the system payoff for everything they've earned — restraint, sacrifice, character. The Grove isn't giving them power because they're strong. It's giving them power because they've proven they'll bleed for it.

**Training Montage (Compressed — 2-3 scenes covering Days 3-5)**

Kenji's Training:
- Works with the Grove's combat druids to refine Iron-Oak Iaido for corridor fighting.
- New Skill: **Rootline Riposte** — after a parry, the Grove's root network amplifies the counter-strike through the ground, hitting enemies at range. Only works on Grove-connected soil (or soil Lyra has purified).
- Upgrades the Leafblade Nodachi — the druids reinforce it with Heart-Tree sap, increasing its anti-corruption damage.
- Kenji's total skill loadout by end: Iron-Oak Iaido, Forest-Kata (Bloodline Tempo), Bloodtide Counter, Rootline Riposte.

Lyra's Training:
- Intensive Purifier development with the Grove woman.
- New Spell: **Verdant Purge (Stable)** — controlled corruption cleansing, room-scale, sustainable. The unstable version she used mid-fight is now a reliable tool.
- New Spell: **Rootspeak** — communication through the root network. She can sense corruption density, locate lattice nodes, and coordinate with Kenji at range.
- Enhancement: **Fire Within → Hearthward** — her warmth spell now creates a purified zone that actively repels corruption. Mobile safe room.
- Lyra's total loadout by end: Verdant Latch, Fire Within/Hearthward, Verdant Purge (Stable), Rootspeak, Verdant Snare (Enhanced).

**The Squirrel Druid Returns**
- The chubby druid from Chapter 2 reappears — this time in human form, serious.
- He volunteers to guide them through the cave's back passages. He knows routes Sable may not have fully corrupted yet.
- "I lost my squirrels to this. I'm not losing my Grove too."

**Departure**
- The Grove prepares them. Healing sap. Antivenom reserves. The shadow-moss cloaks enhanced with ward-thread.
- The remaining druids form a ritual circle and channel energy into Kenji and Lyra — a temporary buff that amplifies their Domain Authority for 24 in-game hours.
- The Grove woman's final words: "If you find what's left of them in there… free them. Even if freeing them means ending them."
- Lyra nods. Kenji grips the nodachi.

**End Chapter 6:** Armed, empowered, and carrying the weight of an entire community's grief. Day 5 begins.

## Chapter 7: The Purifier's Road (9–11 scenes)

**Chapter Goal:** Re-enter cave → Lyra systematically purges corruption room by room → they fight through raised druids → reach Sable's inner sanctum → confront Sable directly → the philosophy clash.

### Scene Breakdown:

**Second Descent**
- Different approach. The squirrel druid leads them through a narrow back passage.
- Lyra's Rootspeak lets her map the corruption network in real-time. She can see the lattice like a blueprint now.
- Kenji feels the Domain Authority — the Grove's ward resonates even here, faintly, like a signal booster in hostile territory.

**Room-by-Room Purge**
- Lyra kneels. Palms down. Verdant Purge activates — stable, controlled, devastating.
- The corruption peels back in a radius around her, the lattice severing like cut wires. The walls shift from blight-black to raw stone.
- Kenji guards the perimeter. Raised creatures stumble into the purified zone and *malfunction* — the lattice that controls them is gone. They collapse into inert husks.
- This is the strategy working. Slow, methodical, unstoppable.
- Sable notices. The cave *reacts* — walls thickening, new creatures deploying, the lattice rebuilding behind them.
- It becomes a race: Lyra purging forward, Sable rebuilding behind, Kenji holding the line between.

**Raised Druid Gauntlet**
- Sable deploys her remaining raised druids — three at once.
- Each one fights with a different corrupted Grove technique: one uses blighted roots, one uses toxic fog (corrupted Fire Within), one uses bone-thorn projectiles.
- Kenji faces two. Lyra faces one while maintaining the purge zone.
- Kenji's Rootline Riposte shines — the purified ground beneath his feet channels Grove energy into counter-strikes that disrupt the raised druids' lattice connection.
- Lyra faces her druid and does something different. Instead of killing it, she *purges* it. She pours Verdant Purge directly into the corrupted druid, burning the lattice out of its body.
- The druid collapses — and for one breath, its eyes are green again. Clear. It looks at Lyra and mouths something. Then it dies.
- Lyra doesn't cry. But something in her goes quiet and hard.

**The Inner Sanctum**
- They breach the final chamber.
- The Blight Nexus is here — a massive organic structure, part root, part bone, part crystal, pulsing with the same lattice pattern they've been seeing since Chapter 1. It's the server. The mainframe. The source.
- And sitting in front of it, cross-legged like a queen on a throne made of the world's worst code, is Sable.

**Sable in Person**
- She's not what they expected.
- Young. Dark-skinned. Natural hair cropped close. Simple clothes — not corrupted armor, not a villain's cape. She looks like someone who works from home and forgot to eat lunch.
- But her eyes are wrong. Pale lattice-light flickers in her pupils, like the Blight is using her retinas as a monitor.
- She's calm. Almost tired.

**The Philosophy Clash**
- Sable talks to them. Not a villain monologue — a debate.
- She explains her worldview: the Grove is a gated community. Nature magic locked behind moral tests. Who decides who's "worthy"? The same kind of people who decide who gets healthcare in the real world. The Blight doesn't judge. It gives power to whoever claims it.
- Lyra pushes back: "It's not giving. It's *overwriting.* You're not freeing anyone — you're reformatting them into your system."
- Sable: "And the Grove doesn't? You 'offer and request.' You 'pay with your body.' That's a subscription with extra steps."
- Kenji (cutting through both): "The difference is consent. The Grove asked us. The Blight didn't ask those druids."
- Sable's expression flickers — the first crack. She didn't think about the raised druids as people. She thought about them as *resources.* The Blight is already in her reasoning.
- She realizes it. For one second, you can see the human behind the lattice-light.
- Then the second passes, and she chooses the Nexus.
- "I can't stop now," she says. "If I let go, it all collapses. Including me."

**The Fight**
- Sable doesn't stand up and swing a sword. She activates the Nexus.
- The chamber transforms. Corruption erupts from every surface. The lattice goes active — aggressive, shaping the environment into a weapon. Walls close. Floor opens. Bone-coral spikes erupt.
- Sable commands it all from the center, eyes fully lattice-lit, no longer entirely a person.
- Kenji fights the terrain. Lyra fights the corruption itself — purging zones for Kenji to stand on, severing lattice connections as fast as Sable rebuilds them.
- The Nexus is the target. If they destroy it, the network dies. But Sable is the Nexus's operator — she's bonded to it. Destroying it might kill her.
- This matters because Sable is Unbound too. No respawn. This is real death.

**End Chapter 7:** The battle is in progress. Cliffhanger mid-fight — Sable escalates, the Nexus overcharges, the cave begins to collapse. The stakes are maximum.

## Chapter 8: The Unbound Choice (8–10 scenes)

**Chapter Goal:** Climax of the Sable fight → moral choice → resolution → consequences → return to Grove → the week ends → wake up.

### Scene Breakdown:

**The Escalation**
- Sable overcharges the Nexus. The cave becomes a living weapon — every surface corrupted, every shadow a threat.
- The squirrel druid holds a retreat corridor open, fighting off blighted creatures with everything he has. "Go! I didn't come back to watch you die!"
- Kenji and Lyra push through to the Nexus core.

**Kenji's Moment**
- Kenji faces Sable's final defense — a corrupted version of the cave itself, shaped into a massive guardian construct made of bone-coral and blighted root.
- This is his final exam. Every skill he's earned fires in sequence: Iron-Oak Iaido reads the openings, Bloodline Tempo chains his counters, Bloodtide Counter activates when the construct targets Lyra and he intercepts, Rootline Riposte channels the purified ground into devastating counter-strikes.
- He doesn't just fight. He *dismantles.* Reading the construct the way he read the voles, the snake, the raised druids. Every fight taught him something. This is the synthesis.
- The construct falls.

**Lyra's Moment**
- The path to the Nexus is clear.
- Lyra places her palms on the Nexus itself.
- Verdant Purge at maximum output. Domain Authority channeling the Grove's full power through her. The Nexus screams — the lattice fighting back with everything it has.
- Lyra holds the line. The corruption peels away layer by layer.
- But underneath, she finds Sable's tether — the bond between the player and the Nexus. If Lyra purges it completely, the backlash will kill Sable. Unbound death. Permanent.

**The Choice**
- Lyra hesitates.
- Kenji sees it. "Lyra?"
- Lyra's voice shakes. "If I finish this, she dies. For real."
- Kenji looks at Sable — suspended in the lattice now, barely conscious, the human almost invisible behind the corruption.
- The system doesn't offer a clean option. There's no "spare the boss" button.
- But Lyra is a Purifier. She doesn't just destroy corruption. She *separates* it.
- She tries something no one's ever done: purge the Nexus while preserving Sable's tether. Burn the system, save the user.
- It costs her. Everything she has. Domain Authority draining, Overreach threshold screaming, her body shaking with the effort of performing magical surgery on a system that wants to kill her.
- Kenji stands over both of them, nodachi drawn, defending against the last desperate spasms of the dying corruption network.

**The Nexus Falls**
- The lattice shatters. The repeating pattern breaks. The corruption network goes offline — across the cave, across the ravine, across the Taiga.
- The Blight static that has been in Kenji's ears since Chapter 1 goes silent.
- For the first time, the Whispering Taiga doesn't whisper.

**Sable's Fate**
- Sable survives. Barely.
- She's stripped of the Blightweaver class. The Nexus is gone. Her power is gone.
- She's just a player now. Unbound. Level nothing. Standing in the ruins of her kingdom in clothes that look like they haven't rendered properly.
- She looks at Lyra with something Kenji can't read — gratitude, hatred, confusion.
- "You could have killed me."
- Lyra: "I could have."
- Sable: "Why didn't you?"
- Lyra doesn't answer right away. Then: "Because the system that rewards character doesn't get to be the one that makes me a killer."
- Sable leaves. Not as an ally. Not as a friend. As an unresolved thread — someone who was right about some things, wrong about others, and now has to figure out who she is without the power she defined herself by.

**Return to the Grove**
- They walk back through a forest that is already changing. Color returning to bark. Birds in trees that were silent for weeks. The frost-line on the ground is gone — replaced by actual sunlight.
- The Grove welcomes them as heroes. The remaining druids perform a ceremony.
- The Heart-Tree blooms — first time in a long time.
- Kenji and Lyra are formally recognized as Grove Wardens — not just Claimants. Owners. Stewards. The real thing.
- The Grove woman smiles for the first time. "You did what we couldn't."
- Kenji: "We did what you taught us to."

**The Last Night (Day 7)**
- Their final in-game evening before the pod timer runs out.
- They sit at the base of the Heart-Tree. The Grove is quiet. The Taiga is quiet.
- No UI notifications. No threats. Just two people who walked into hell together and came out holding the same things they went in with — each other.
- The romantic tension that's been building for seven in-game days finally gets a moment to breathe. Not a resolution. A promise. A look. A hand that doesn't let go.
- Kenji's internal: `Seven days. We fought monsters, survived poison, stormed a necromancer's fortress, and saved a forest. And the thing I'm going to remember most is the way she looks at me when she thinks I'm not paying attention.`

**The Wake-Up**
- The pod timer hits zero.
- The gel hisses. The neural link disengages.
- Kenji opens his eyes in the apartment. Real world. 2050. Managed Comfort. Subscription air.
- Eight hours have passed.
- He turns his head. Lyra is already looking at him from her pod, eyes bright — no amber glow, just human warmth.
- "Good morning, city boy."
- Kenji smiles. The first real one in years.
- "Good morning, Warden."

**End of Book 1.**

## Book 2 Seeds (Planted, Not Resolved)

- The Blight Nexus was ancient infrastructure — **someone built it long before Sable found it.** Who? Why?
- Sable is alive, Unbound, and now knows the Grove's secrets from the inside. Is she an ally? A future enemy? A wildcard?
- The whale's faction lost people in the cave. Corporate players don't forget losses — they send accountants, then armies.
- Kenji and Lyra now *own* territory. In a game where ownership is real economy, that makes them targets for every player-king and faction in Omnia.
- The 24:1 time dilation means their real-world lives continue. Jobs. Bills. The managed comfort they're trying to escape. Book 2 could explore what happens when your game-world success starts mattering more than your real-world survival.
